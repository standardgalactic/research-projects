Complex systems—from galaxies to neural networks—exhibit dynamics governed by the interplay of local interactions
and global constraints. RSVP theory provides a mathematical foundation for describing such systems through:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]

\section{Core Mathematical Framework}
\subsection{RSVP Field Equations}
The fundamental field equations couple scalar, vector, and entropy terms:
\begin{align}
\partial_t \Phi + \nabla \cdot (\Phi \mathbf{v}) &= -\lambda \dot{\Sigma}, \\
\partial_t S + \nabla \cdot (S \mathbf{v}) &= \dot{\Sigma}, \\
\partial_t \mathbf{v} &= -\nabla R + \nabla \times \mathbf{T},
\end{align}
where $\mathbf{T}$ denotes torsion or lamphron-lamphrodyne smoothing.
These arise from variational minimization of the effective potential
\[
\mathcal{L} = \tfrac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2 - \Phi + \lambda S,

The stress-energy tensor for RSVP fields is
\[
T^{\mu\nu} = \Phi u^\mu u^\nu + g^{\mu\nu}(\lambda S - \Phi),

\[
\partial_\mu T^{\mu\nu} = 0.

I. Fundamental Definitions & Mathematical Rigor
Core Field Definitions
What is the domain of definition for Φ, v, and S?
Are these fields defined on a Riemannian manifold, Minkowski spacetime, or an abstract configuration space? What regularity class (C^k, Sobolev H^s)?
How is entropy S(x,t) operationally defined at a point?
Traditional entropy is a global, ensemble property. What coarse-graining, averaging volume, or microstate counting justifies a
local
entropy field?
What are the units and dimensionality of each field?
Does Φ have units of energy density [J/m³]? Is S dimensionless or per unit volume [J/(K·m³)]? Do v components have units of velocity [m/s]?
What is λ physically?
Is it a coupling constant, a Lagrange multiplier, a temperature-like parameter? Why does it appear both as an entropy weight in R = Φ - λS and as a coefficient in the field equations?
The "Plenum" Concept
What does "plenum" mean here?
Is this aether, a physical medium, a mathematical background space, or metaphorical language? How does it differ from the vacuum or spacetime itself?
Why "Relativistic"?
The equations shown are non-relativistic (∂_t + v·∇ structure). Where does special or general relativity enter? Is T^μν truly a 4D stress-energy tensor?
Entropy Production Rate
How is Σ̇ computed from first principles?
You reference "tiling and torsion operators" but never define them. What microscopic dynamics or statistical ensemble generates Σ̇?
Can Σ̇ be negative?
If so, does this violate the second law? If not, how is positivity enforced mathematically?
What is the relationship between ∇S and Σ̇?
Equation (2) says ∂_t S + ∇·(Sv) = Σ̇. But also Σ̇ = ∫(∇S)·v dV. Are these consistent?
II. Field Equation Inconsistencies
Conservation Laws
Are equations (1-3) dimensionally consistent?
Check each term in ∂_t Φ + ∇·(Φv) = -λΣ̇. If Φ is energy density and v is velocity, LHS has units [J/(m³·s)]. What are the units of λΣ̇?
Energy-momentum conservation
: You claim ∂_μ T^μν = 0, but equation (1) has a source term -λΣ̇. This implies energy is
not
conserved. Which is it?
Entropy continuity contradiction
: Equation (2) is ∂_t S + ∇·(Sv) = Σ̇, but earlier you wrote ∂_t S + ∇·(Φv) = Σ̇. Which field (S or Φ) advects the entropy?
Vector Field Evolution
What determines the torsion field T?
Equation (3) introduces ∇×T without defining T. Is it a derived quantity from Φ and S, or an independent field?
Why does ∂_t v = -∇R + ∇×T have no damping or inertial terms?
Real fluids/fields have viscosity, mass, etc. Is this a zero-mass, inviscid limit?
What boundary conditions apply?
For the differential equations to be well-posed, you need BCs at spatial infinity or domain boundaries. What are they?
III. Variational Structure & Lagrangian
The Action Principle
How does L = ½|v|² - Φ + λS yield equations (1-3)?
Show the Euler-Lagrange derivation explicitly. What is the action S = ∫L d⁴x?
Is this Lagrangian relativistic?
It looks like a non-relativistic kinetic energy minus a potential. Where are the metric g_μν and proper time?
What symmetries does this action possess?
By Noether's theorem, symmetries → conservation laws. What are they?
BV Formalism
What is the gauge symmetry?
You invoke the BV master equation {S,S}=0 and ΔS=0, but don't specify the gauge transformations being fixed.
What are the ghost fields?
BV formalism extends phase space with ghosts. What are they here, and how do they couple to Φ, S, v?
How does "entropy-conserving transformation" work?
Entropy production Σ̇ ≥ 0 seems incompatible with symmetries that conserve S.
IV. Critical Transition & Phase Structure
The Critical Point λ_c
How is λ_c = 0.42 ± 0.03 derived?
Is this from simulation, analytic bifurcation analysis, or a fit? What observable quantity diverges or vanishes at λ_c?
What is the order of the phase transition?
First-order (discontinuity), second-order (critical exponents), something else?
Why is ∂R/∂λ = 0 the criticality condition?
R = Φ - λS is a linear function of λ, so ∂R/∂λ = -S. This just says entropy vanishes—is that physical?
Lyapunov Stability
Is ΔR_k a true Lyapunov function?
Lyapunov functions must be positive definite and monotonically decreasing. Does ΔR_k satisfy V(x) > 0 and dV/dt < 0?
What does "Lyapunov proxy" mean?
Is this an approximation to the largest Lyapunov exponent λ_max, or something else?

Upwind, central, Lax-Wendroff? Stability depends critically on this choice.
