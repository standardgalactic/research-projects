Lab 04 – Attractor Switch

A double-well potential with a tunable barrier.

State \(x\) evolves in a symmetric double-well potential. A central barrier separates left and right wells. You can adjust barrier height and noise. High barriers imprison the system in one well; low barriers allow transitions—analogous to whether a system can meaningfully switch between competing attractors.

Reading it: the canvas shows \(x\) over time as a horizontal trace. Top half is positive (right well), bottom half is negative (left well). When barrier height is large relative to noise, the trace hugs one side. As you lower the barrier or raise noise, rare transitions occur—RSVP-style “attractor crossing.”
