Lab 02 – Mediator Stratum

Signal from below, capture in the middle, attenuation above.

A 1D chain of sites is divided into three zones: lower (donkey), middle (mediator), and upper (lion/fox). Each time step, the lower site injects a pulse. Couplings control how much of that signal reaches the middle and then the top. Tuning the mediator coupling explores how “Lorenzo-like” nodes can either transmit or absorb change.

Reading it: each column is a time step; rows are strata (bottom = lower, middle = mediator, top = upper). Brightness encodes signal strength. Heavy mediator damping with low upward coupling mimics a captured middle class that absorbs unrest instead of transmitting it.
