Lab 01 – Curvature Field

A particle sliding on a scalar potential with adjustable curvature.

The grid below represents a simple scalar potential \( \Phi(x, y) = k (x^2 + y^2) \). A single particle moves according to gradient descent. Increasing curvature (k) steepens the well, making escape more difficult.

Reading it: lighter cells are higher potential, darker cells are lower. The particle (bright square) is “falling” into darker regions. With low curvature, noise kicks can push it outward. With high curvature, trajectories lock into the center—a toy version of “steep hierarchy wells.”
