Characters & Recurring Roles

Humans, animals, mediators, and metaphysical entities that echo across decks and dimensions.

Monks (Human World)

  • Brother Tommaso – Bruno’s closest friend. A cheerful skeptic who laughs easily, obeys reluctantly, and later becomes the informal keeper of Bruno’s stories.
  • Brother Lorenzo – The model monk, gentle and diplomatic. His dream-self becomes Lorenzo the Llama, embodying the middle stratum of the Ark.
  • Brother Daniele – An anxious idealist who loves rules but loves Bruno more. Torn between safety and truth, he is almost always the last to leave forbidden gatherings.
  • Brother Agnolo – Overly devout novice, takes rules literally. Oscillates between admiration and fear of Bruno; maps onto fox-like boundary-policing in allegories.
  • Brother Matteo – Nearly silent; observes everything. Later revealed to have memorized more of Bruno’s work than anyone suspected.
  • Brother Crispin (The Librarian) – Guardian of the monastery’s books, suspicious but secretly impressed by Bruno. Ark counterpart: Pica the fox archivist.

Ark Animals

Lorenzo the Llama

The core mediator figure: stands between donkeys and foxes/lions, translating grievances into procedure. Over time he becomes a vector field embodying the Ark’s middle stratum—first softening curvature, then captured by it, and finally transcending the Ark altogether.

Donkey Chorus

  • Grano – Elder storyteller, cynical but loyal. First to recognize the allegorical power of Bruno’s tales.
  • Anice – Young idealist who eventually sees the horizon beyond the Ark in Episode 11.
  • Bitto – Overworked, exhausted, and unintentionally funny; his complaints form the backbone of the Donkey Gospel.

Fox Delegation

  • Vulpes – Lead fox and master of procedural delay. Architect of the administrative coup in “Foxes at the Helm.”
  • Saffron – A fox sympathetic to the donkeys but trapped by loyalty and fear of losing status.
  • Pica – Archivist fox; animal-world counterpart to Brother Crispin. Oversees confiscated texts and burns the Donkey Gospel.

Lions

  • Aurus – Primary lion ruler, increasingly weary of his ceremonial role. Attempts to descend but is blocked by curvature in “The Lion’s Dream.”
  • Regulus – A more introspective lion, aware of the hollowness of power but unable to renounce it.

Other Ark Figures

  • Noah – Conflicted architect, caught between divine blueprints and structural impossibility. Appears most strongly in “Noah’s Revision.”
  • Deck 0 Entities – Silent, uncanny beings in the unseen layer that absorb suppressed rebellion and sorrow.

Cosmic & Metaphysical Figures

  • Bureau Archivists – Celestial functionaries in “The Bureau of Arks” who treat world-geometry as paperwork.
  • The Horizon – A quasi-personified boundary in Episode 11, representing the edge of the Ark manifold.
  • Trial Judges – Lion-fox hybrids in the finale, performing Bruno’s condemnation in an endlessly looping play.
