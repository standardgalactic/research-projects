Lab 08 – Ethical Gradient

A biased potential landscape; a single agent descends with noise.

The moral potential \(U(x,y)\) is a simple bowl with an added skew term: \[ U(x,y) = \tfrac{1}{2}(x^2 + y^2) + s x. \] The particle follows noisy gradient descent with learning rate \(\alpha\).

Reading it: the background is the potential landscape; the bright dot is the agent. With strong skew and low noise, it slides deterministically into a biased basin. Higher noise or smaller \(\alpha\) make wandering and local detours more visible—an RSVP toy for how “alignment” is just descent in someone’s chosen landscape.
