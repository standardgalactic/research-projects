Lab 05 – Gradient Weave

Scalar Φ as brightness, vector v as arrows: weaving flow and density.

This lab couples a scalar potential \(\Phi\) and a vector field \(\mathbf{v}\) on a coarse grid: \[ \dot{\Phi} = -\nabla\cdot\mathbf{v}, \quad \dot{\mathbf{v}} = -\lambda \nabla \Phi - \nu\,\mathbf{v}. \] You can tune the coupling \(\lambda\), the viscosity \(\nu\), and a discrete feedback delay.

Reading it: background brightness is \(\Phi\); arrows show \(\mathbf{v}\). High \(\lambda\) with low \(\nu\) tends to generate sharp weaving flows and unstable vortices. High \(\nu\) damps everything into a smooth green fog. In RSVP terms: the plenum either weaves gradients into motion or smothers them into equilibrium.
